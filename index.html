How I work

Every project runs on the same discipline, applied before the results are in rather than reverse-engineered to fit them:

  • Thresholds pre-registered before seeing results, so a strategy passes or fails against a standard it can't move.
  • Deflated Sharpe reported alongside the naive Sharpe, accounting for the number of trials behind any headline figure.
  • Newey-West HAC t-statistics for overlapping returns, instead of standard errors that pretend the observations are independent.
  • Purged k-fold with embargo to keep training and test sets from leaking across the gap.
  • Capacity simulation, so a Sharpe comes with the honest size of book it survives at.
  • Negative results shipped, not buried — the write-up reports what didn't work as carefully as what did.

Two of the three strategies here are honest nulls. That is deliberate. A method that only ever produces winners isn't a method — it's a filter on the reporting. Showing the nulls in full, with the diagnostics that make them interesting, is the point.
